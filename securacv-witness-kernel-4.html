Overview

Three design constraints

Every decision serves one goal: produce useful security events without creating a surveillance substrate.

Object detection produces a class label and region ‚Äî no identity, no persistence, and no cross-event linkage.

Who this is for

Security without a surveillance substrate

Witness-kernel is designed for environments where video evidence may be necessary, but unrestricted surveillance is unacceptable.

  • Shared buildings, housing co-ops, and community spaces
  • Workplaces that need incident accountability without employee tracking
  • Developers and researchers building privacy-constrained security systems
  • Anyone who believes capability limits are stronger than policy promises

Event-based output

Sealed log of discrete events. No continuous video export API.

No biometric code

Face, plate, gait recognition absent from codebase. Not disabled ‚Äî missing.

Quorum export

Raw footage requires N-of-M trustee approval. No single administrator, user, or account can extract raw footage unilaterally ‚Äî by design.

Different goals, not better or worse

HomeKit and Frigate are excellent at what they do. If you need face recognition, plate reading, or easy video export ‚Äî use them. Witness-kernel exists for cases where those capabilities are liabilities, not features. Encryption alone does not prevent misuse ‚Äî only missing capabilities do.

How It Works

Enforced constraints

I

Private frame data

RawFrame.data has no public accessor. Modules receive InferenceView only.

II

Token-gated export

export_for_vault() requires BreakGlassToken from quorum authorization.

III

Coarsened timestamps

10-minute buckets at capture. Precise timestamps not stored.

IV

Hash-chained log

Each event includes hash of previous. Tampering breaks verification.

V

Single-use tokens

Break-glass tokens are envelope-bound, consumed on use, expire within bucket.

VI

Forward-only rulesets

ReprocessGuard blocks new rules on historical data.
